## How Slot Machines Work

### Random Number Generator (RNG)
Every spin outcome is determined by a Random Number Generator — a computer algorithm that produces unpredictable results. Key facts:
- Each spin is completely independent of previous spins
- There is no "hot" or "cold" machine — past results don't affect future results
- The RNG runs continuously, even when nobody is playing
- The exact millisecond you press spin determines the outcome

**What this means:** No strategy, pattern recognition, or timing can predict or influence outcomes. Anyone claiming otherwise is wrong.

## Return to Player (RTP)

RTP is the percentage of wagered money a slot pays back to players over millions of spins.

- **96% RTP** = for every $100 wagered, $96 is returned on average
- **4% house edge** = the casino keeps 4% on average

### Important Caveats
- RTP is calculated over millions of spins — it's a long-run statistical average
- In any single session, results can vary wildly from the stated RTP
- A 96% RTP slot can pay out 200% in one session and 10% in another
- Higher RTP is better but doesn't guarantee better short-term results

### RTP Ranges
| RTP | Classification |
|-----|---------------|
| 98%+ | Excellent |
| 96-98% | Good |
| 94-96% | Average |
| Below 94% | Poor |

Most Stake slots fall in the 96-97% range. Some Stake Originals have higher RTP.

## Volatility (Variance)

Volatility describes how a slot distributes its payouts.

### High Volatility
- Infrequent wins but larger payouts when they hit
- Long dry spells between wins
- Balance swings dramatically
- Higher potential for big wins
- Examples: Money Train 3, Deadwood, San Quentin, Gates of Olympus

**Best for:** Content creation (dramatic swings), players with larger bankrolls, bonus buy sessions

### Medium Volatility
- Balance between frequency and size of wins
- More consistent gameplay
- Examples: Book of Dead, Gonzo's Quest, Wolf Gold

**Best for:** Longer sessions, moderate bankrolls

### Low Volatility
- Frequent small wins
- Balance stays relatively stable
- Lower maximum win potential
- Examples: Starburst, Joker's Jewels, Fire Joker

**Best for:** Extended play time, smaller bankrolls, casual play

## Key Slot Mechanics

### Paylines vs Ways to Win
- **Paylines**: Fixed lines across the reels where matching symbols pay. Traditional slots have 10-25 paylines
- **Ways to Win**: Any matching symbols in adjacent reels pay, regardless of position. Typically 243, 1024, or more ways
- **Megaways**: Dynamic reel system where the number of symbols per reel changes each spin, creating up to 117,649 ways to win

### Cascading/Tumbling Reels
Winning symbols disappear and new symbols fall to replace them, potentially creating chain wins from a single spin. Common in Gates of Olympus, Sweet Bonanza, Gonzo's Quest.

### Multipliers
Values that multiply your win. Can be:
- Fixed (always 2x, 5x, etc.)
- Progressive (increases with each cascade or during bonus)
- Random (applied randomly to wins)

### Wild Symbols
Substitute for other symbols to complete winning combinations. Variants include:
- **Expanding Wilds**: Expand to cover entire reel
- **Sticky Wilds**: Remain in place for multiple spins
- **Walking Wilds**: Move one position each spin

### Scatter Symbols
Trigger bonus features regardless of position on reels. Usually 3+ scatters trigger free spins or bonus rounds.

## Bonus Features

### Free Spins
The most common bonus feature. Triggered by scatter symbols. During free spins:
- No additional cost per spin
- Often include enhanced multipliers
- Can be retriggered in some slots
- Where most big wins occur

### Bonus Buy
Pay a premium (typically 50-100x your bet) to immediately enter the bonus round, skipping the base game.
- Costs more upfront but guarantees bonus entry
- Popular for content creation — no waiting for organic triggers
- Not available on all platforms or in all regions
- Effective cost per bonus is higher than organic triggers

### Pick Bonus
Choose from options to reveal prizes. Outcome is predetermined by RNG at the moment of trigger.

### Wheel Bonus
Spin a wheel for prizes, multipliers, or jackpot entries.

## Betting Strategy

### Bankroll Management
There is no betting strategy that overcomes the house edge. However, bankroll management affects how long you play and your variance exposure.

**Flat betting**: Same bet every spin. Maximises session length, consistent variance exposure.

**Increasing bets**: Higher risk, can lead to faster bankroll depletion. No mathematical advantage.

**Bet sizing relative to bankroll:**
- For extended sessions: bet 0.5-1% of bankroll per spin
- For bonus buy sessions: each buy costs 50-100x bet, plan accordingly
- For high volatility slots: smaller bets relative to bankroll to survive dry spells

### The Gambler's Fallacy
The belief that past results influence future outcomes. It doesn't. A slot that hasn't paid in 100 spins is not "due" — each spin is independent.
