# Sorkin: How to Write a Cold Open for a Stream

## Category: streaming/storytelling
## Tags: aaron_sorkin, cold_open, hook, opening, structure, storytelling, streaming

A cold open establishes conflict before the audience has time to disengage. It answers the question "why should I keep watching?" in the first 30 seconds.

**Formula:** State your intention + name the obstacle + raise the stakes.

**Example cold open (gambling stream):**
"Tonight I'm turning $300 into $3000. I've been down $800 this week. This is the comeback session. If I don't hit it tonight, I'm taking a break. Let's go."

That's 3 sentences. Intention ($3000). Obstacle (down $800). Stakes (break if it fails). The audience now has a reason to stay.

**What NOT to do:**
- "Hey guys, what's up, how's everyone doing" — no hook, no stakes, no reason to stay
- Starting with dead air while you set up OBS
- Explaining what you're about to do instead of just doing it

**Rule:** The cold open is a promise. The rest of the stream is the delivery.
