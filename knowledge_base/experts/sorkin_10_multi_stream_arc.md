# Sorkin: Multi-Stream Arc Design

## Category: streaming/storytelling
## Tags: aaron_sorkin, arc, multi_stream, series, challenge, narrative, long_form, streaming

Sorkin designs season-long arcs where individual episodes serve the larger story. Each episode has its own complete arc while advancing the season arc. Applied to streaming: individual streams are episodes, a challenge series is a season.

**The three arc levels:**
- **Episode** = individual stream. Complete arc: cold open, rising action, climax, resolution.
- **Season** = a series of streams with a connecting theme. Examples: bankroll challenge, game series, comeback arc, monthly goal.
- **Series** = the overall streaming career. The long story of who you are as a streamer.

**Why multi-stream arcs matter:**
They give long-term viewers a reason to return. A viewer who watched Stream 1 of a $500-to-$5000 challenge has a stake in Stream 10. They're invested in the outcome. New viewers can enter at any point but are rewarded for going back.

**How to design a season arc:**

1. **Establish the premise** (Stream 1): "I'm starting with $500. The goal is $5000. No time limit. I stream until I hit it or go broke."
2. **Track progress visibly** (every stream): Show the running total. Reference where you started. Build the narrative.
3. **Create turning points** (mid-season): A big loss that threatens the challenge. A comeback. A near-miss on the goal.
4. **Pay it off** (final stream): Win or lose, the arc resolves. The audience gets closure.

**Example season arc structure:**
- Stream 1: $500 start. First session. Establish the rules.
- Streams 2-5: Progress and setbacks. Build the narrative.
- Stream 6: Major loss. Down to $200. The challenge is in danger.
- Stream 7: Comeback. Back to $600.
- Stream 8: Hit $2000. Halfway there.
- Stream 9: Big loss. Back to $800.
- Stream 10: Final push. Hit $5000 or go broke.

**Template for announcing a season arc:**
"Starting today, I'm doing [CHALLENGE]. The rules are [RULES]. I'll track it every stream. [CONSEQUENCE IF SUCCESS]. [CONSEQUENCE IF FAILURE]. Let's find out which one it is."
