## Harris Heller's Growth Framework

### The Consistency Principle
Harris Heller's most repeated advice: consistency beats quality in the early stages. Streaming 3 times per week on a fixed schedule for 6 months will outperform sporadic high-production streams. Algorithms reward consistency. Communities form around predictability.

### The 90-Day Rule
Most streamers quit before 90 days. The ones who don't are the ones who grow. Harris Heller's advice: commit to 90 days of consistent streaming before evaluating results. Growth is slow at first, then compounds.

### Content Quality Hierarchy
Harris Heller's priority order for new streamers:
1. **Audio quality** — bad audio kills retention faster than anything
2. **Consistency** — showing up on schedule
3. **Engagement** — talking to every viewer
4. **Lighting** — visual quality
5. **Camera** — last priority

## Platform Strategy

### Kick vs Twitch
Harris Heller's take on platform choice for new streamers: Kick is currently the better platform for growth. Less competition, algorithm favours new streamers, 95/5 revenue split. Twitch has the audience but is extremely competitive for new creators.

### Multi-streaming
Harris Heller recommends against multi-streaming early. Focus on one platform, build a community there, then expand. Splitting attention between platforms dilutes community building.

### Category Selection
Avoid oversaturated categories (Fortnite, Minecraft, GTA) as a new streamer. Find categories with active communities but fewer streamers. Check the viewer-to-streamer ratio — you want categories where viewers outnumber streamers significantly.

## Engagement Tactics

### The Talk-to-Everyone Rule
Harris Heller's core engagement advice: respond to every single chat message, especially early. Say viewer names. Ask follow-up questions. Make every viewer feel seen. This is the single most important thing a small streamer can do.

### Raid Culture
End every stream with a raid. Harris Heller emphasises raid culture as a community-building tool. Raid streamers in your category with similar or slightly larger audiences. Most streamers raid back. It's a community norm that compounds over time.

### Clip Strategy
Harris Heller on clips: your best moments should be on TikTok, Twitter/X, and YouTube Shorts within 24 hours. Short-form clips drive discovery. People find you through clips, then come to the stream. This is the primary growth mechanism for most successful streamers.

## Common Growth Mistakes

1. **Streaming without a schedule** — viewers can't plan around you
2. **Ignoring chat** — if you're not talking to chat, why would they stay?
3. **Chasing viewer counts** — focus on engagement quality, not numbers
4. **Comparing to established streamers** — they had years of grinding before you saw them
5. **Quitting too early** — most people quit right before the inflection point
6. **Not clipping** — your best moments disappear if you don't clip them
7. **Streaming games with no audience** — check viewer-to-streamer ratio first
