# Sorkin: A-Story B-Story C-Story for Streams

## Category: streaming/storytelling
## Tags: aaron_sorkin, structure, abc_story, narrative, engagement, pacing, streaming

Every Sorkin show runs three simultaneous storylines. When one goes quiet, another carries the audience. A stream with only one story dies the moment that story goes quiet.

**The three layers:**
- **A-Story** — what's happening RIGHT NOW. The current spin, the current game, the current moment.
- **B-Story** — the session goal building in the background. The challenge, the target, the comeback.
- **C-Story** — the long arc. The overall P&L, the streak, the community narrative.

**Example in practice:**
- A-Story: "We're on Gates of Olympus, base game, waiting for scatters"
- B-Story: "We need to hit $2000 tonight to break even on the week"
- C-Story: "This is stream 5 of the $500-to-$5000 challenge"

When the A-Story is dead (no hits, waiting), the B-Story carries the audience ("we're still $400 short of the target"). When the B-Story resolves, the C-Story gives it meaning ("that's $1200 profit on the challenge so far").

**Template for a 3-hour stream:**
- Open: establish all three stories in the first 5 minutes
- Hour 1: A-Story drives, B-Story builds
- Hour 2: B-Story becomes the A-Story, C-Story becomes visible
- Hour 3: All three converge toward resolution

**What NOT to do:**
- Streaming with no stated goal (no B-Story)
- Never referencing the session arc (no C-Story)
- Treating every spin as isolated (no narrative continuity)
