# Sorkin: Eliminating Dead Air

## Category: streaming/storytelling
## Tags: aaron_sorkin, dead_air, pacing, commentary, narration, streaming, engagement

Sorkin never lets a scene breathe without purpose. Every moment of silence in his scripts is intentional and brief. Dead air in streaming is the equivalent of a scene that goes nowhere — it bleeds energy and loses viewers.

**The three types of dead air in streaming:**
1. **Waiting dead air** — spinning with nothing to say
2. **Transition dead air** — silence between games or segments
3. **Processing dead air** — reacting to something but not speaking

**How to fill each type:**

**Waiting dead air:**
Narrate what you're watching for. "If this lands on three scatters, we're in the bonus. Watch the middle reel." This gives the audience something to focus on and creates micro-tension.

**Transition dead air:**
Use button lines (see Sorkin: Button Lines). Close the previous segment, state what's coming next. "That game is done. We're moving to Money Train 3. Different energy, same goal."

**Processing dead air:**
React out loud. Even "okay... okay... that's interesting" is better than silence. Verbalise your thought process. "I'm deciding whether to buy the bonus or grind the base game. The base game has been cold for 20 minutes. I'm buying."

**The commentary rule:**
Never describe what the audience can already see. Add information. "The multiplier is at 47x" is describing. "If this multiplier hits 100x before the spins run out, we're looking at a $4000 win" is adding information.

**Pace matching:**
Match your commentary pace to the action. Slow spins = faster talk to maintain energy. Fast action = let the visuals breathe, react sharply.
