# Sorkin: Tension and Release Cycle

## Category: streaming/storytelling
## Tags: aaron_sorkin, tension, release, pacing, engagement, bonus_buy, streaming

Sorkin's scenes build tension through escalating stakes and release it through resolution — then immediately introduce new tension. The audience never fully relaxes.

**The cycle:** Build → Peak → Release → Immediately introduce new tension

**Applied to a bonus buy:**
1. BUILD: "This is the last bonus buy of the night. $200 on the line. If this doesn't hit, we're done."
2. PEAK: Narrate the bonus loading, the first few spins, the multipliers building
3. RELEASE: React genuinely to the outcome — win or lose
4. NEW TENSION: "Okay. That was $400. We need $600 more to hit the target. One more game."

**The mistake most streamers make:** They release tension (big win) and then let the energy drop. Dead air. Chat goes quiet. Viewers leave. The release must be followed immediately by new tension.

**Pacing rule:** The gap between release and new tension should be under 10 seconds. React, celebrate or absorb the loss, then immediately pivot to what's next.

**Example of bad pacing:**
Win → "Oh my god that was insane" → silence → "okay so uh... what should I play next?" → dead air

**Example of good pacing:**
Win → "THAT'S what I'm talking about. $800 profit. Now we need to decide — do we lock it in or go for the $1000 target? I say we go for it. One more."
