# Sorkin: Stream Rundown Template

## Category: streaming/storytelling
## Tags: aaron_sorkin, rundown, template, structure, planning, segments, streaming

A rundown is a pre-planned sequence of segments. Sorkin scripts every scene. You don't need to script every spin — but you should know what the shape of your stream looks like before you go live.

**The 3-hour stream rundown template:**

---

**SEGMENT 1: Cold Open + Setup (0-10 min)**
- Cold open: state intention, obstacle, stakes (30-60 seconds)
- Establish the A-Story: what game, what goal
- Hint at the B-Story: the session target
- First game begins

**SEGMENT 2: Rising Action (10-60 min)**
- Build toward first major moment
- Narrate the B-Story building in the background
- First callback opportunity (reference the cold open)
- First climax: a win, a near-miss, or a decision point

**SEGMENT 3: Escalation (60-90 min)**
- Raise the stakes from Segment 2
- B-Story becomes more urgent
- Game switch or strategy change
- Button line to close the segment

**SEGMENT 4: Second Act (90-150 min)**
- B-Story becomes the A-Story
- C-Story (session arc) becomes visible
- Highest tension point of the stream
- Major win or major loss — the turning point

**SEGMENT 5: Resolution (150-180 min)**
- Build toward final resolution
- Pay off the cold open promise
- Close the arc: did you achieve your intention?
- Button line to close the stream

---

**Minimum viable rundown (for shorter streams):**
1. Cold open with intention + stakes
2. Rising action toward one major goal
3. Resolution — win or lose, close the arc

**What NOT to do:**
- Going live with no plan
- Ending a stream without closing the arc
- Switching games randomly without narrative justification
