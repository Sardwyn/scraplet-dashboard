# Sorkin: Callbacks — Rewarding Loyal Viewers

## Category: streaming/storytelling
## Tags: aaron_sorkin, callbacks, continuity, loyal_viewers, community, narrative, streaming

A callback is a reference to something that happened earlier — in the same stream, in a previous stream, or in the community's shared history. Sorkin uses callbacks constantly. They reward attentive viewers and create a sense of shared experience.

**Why callbacks matter:**
They transform passive viewers into invested participants. A viewer who remembers the reference feels seen. A new viewer who doesn't understand it gets curious and wants to know the backstory.

**Types of callbacks in streaming:**

**Same-stream callbacks:**
"Remember when I said this game was dead? That was 40 minutes ago. Look at it now."
"We said we'd stop at $1000 profit. We're at $980. Almost."

**Cross-stream callbacks:**
"Last week I lost $800 on this exact game. Tonight we get it back."
"This is the slot that hit the 2000x three months ago. Chat was insane that night."

**Community callbacks:**
"Wittlebears, you called this game two hours ago. You were right. I owe you one."
"This is the game MsKatastrophic always asks me to play. Tonight's for her."

**How to build callback material:**
State things clearly when they happen so they can be referenced later. "I'm going to remember this moment" is a setup. The callback is the payoff.

**Template:**
"[Time reference] I said [what you said]. [What happened since]. [Callback punchline]."

Example: "Two hours ago I said this game was going to pay. Everyone in chat said I was wrong. The game just paid $1200. I'm not saying I told you so. I'm saying I told you so."
