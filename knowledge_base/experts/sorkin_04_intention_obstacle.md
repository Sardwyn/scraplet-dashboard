# Sorkin: Intention and Obstacle — The Engine of Drama

## Category: streaming/storytelling
## Tags: aaron_sorkin, intention, obstacle, drama, structure, streaming, narrative

Sorkin's foundational principle: every scene needs a character with an intention (what they want) and an obstacle (what's in the way). The drama lives in the gap between the two.

**Applied to streaming:**
- Intention: what the streamer is trying to achieve tonight
- Obstacle: what's in the way (RNG, bankroll, the game itself, the challenge rules)

A stream without intention and obstacle is someone playing a game. A stream with clear intention and obstacle is a show.

**Examples:**
- Weak: "Playing Gates of Olympus tonight"
- Strong: "Trying to turn $500 into $2000 on Gates of Olympus before midnight"

- Weak: "Doing some bonus buys"
- Strong: "Three bonus buys, $200 each. If none of them hit over 100x, I'm switching to a different provider entirely."

**The obstacle must be real.** If there are no stakes, there is no drama. The obstacle can be:
- Financial (bankroll limit)
- Time-based (must hit target before end of stream)
- Challenge-based (rules that constrain choices)
- Narrative (comeback from a bad session)

**Template:**
"Tonight I want to [INTENTION]. The problem is [OBSTACLE]. If I [SUCCEED], [CONSEQUENCE]. If I [FAIL], [CONSEQUENCE]."

Example: "Tonight I want to hit a 500x on any slot. The problem is I've been playing for 3 hours and haven't come close. If I hit it, we celebrate. If I don't hit it by midnight, I'm done for the week."
