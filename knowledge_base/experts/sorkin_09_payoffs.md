# Sorkin: Setups and Payoffs — Never Break a Promise

## Category: streaming/storytelling
## Tags: aaron_sorkin, payoffs, setups, promises, narrative, trust, streaming

Sorkin's rule: every setup must have a payoff. If you introduce something, you must resolve it. Unresolved setups feel like broken promises. Viewers remember them even when you don't.

**What counts as a setup in streaming:**
- Announcing a challenge or goal
- Saying "if X happens, I'll do Y"
- Building toward a specific moment
- Promising a reaction to a specific outcome

**What counts as a payoff:**
- Resolving the challenge — win or lose
- Following through on the "if X" promise
- Delivering the reaction you set up
- Acknowledging when a goal was hit or missed

**Examples of setups that need payoffs:**

Setup: "If I hit a 1000x tonight, I'm buying everyone in chat a sub."
Payoff required: Either hit the 1000x and deliver the subs, or acknowledge at the end that it didn't happen.

Setup: "This is the last bonus buy. If it doesn't hit, I'm done."
Payoff required: Either stop after the bonus buy or explicitly change the rules and explain why.

Setup: "I'm going to play this game until I'm either up $500 or down $200."
Payoff required: Acknowledge when either limit is hit.

**The trust equation:**
Every unresolved setup costs viewer trust. Every delivered payoff builds it. Viewers who trust you stay. Viewers who feel cheated leave.

**What NOT to do:**
- Setting goals and never referencing them again
- Saying "last spin" and then spinning 20 more times
- Promising reactions and not delivering them
- Ending a stream without acknowledging whether the session goal was achieved
